// 04 — Education
Market concepts

Key financial terms explained simply — the building blocks of informed investing.

ROIReturns
Return on Investment measures profit relative to what you put in. A 20% ROI means for every $100 invested, you made $20.
ROI = (Gain − Cost) / Cost × 100
ROEProfitability
Return on Equity tells you how efficiently a company uses shareholder money to generate profit. A high ROE signals a capital-efficient business.
ROE = Net Income / Shareholders' Equity × 100
MWRPerformance
Money-Weighted Return is your personal rate of return — the IRR of all cash flows. Accounts for the timing of every deposit and withdrawal.
MWR = (Final / Initial)^(1/years) − 1
SWRRetirement
Safe Withdrawal Rate — how much you can withdraw annually without running out in 30 years. The "4% rule" comes from the Trinity Study.
SWR = Portfolio Value × 0.04
P/E RatioValuation
Price-to-Earnings compares share price to earnings per share. A high P/E can mean high growth expectations — or an overvalued stock.
P/E = Share Price / Earnings Per Share
REITAsset class
Real Estate Investment Trusts let you invest in property like a stock. By law they distribute 90%+ of taxable income as dividends — high-yield assets.
Dividend Yield = Annual Dividend / Share Price
ETFInstruments
Exchange-Traded Funds hold a basket of assets and trade like a stock. Instant diversification at low cost — VTI holds 3,500+ US stocks in one instrument.
Expense Ratio = Annual Fee / Fund Value
BetaRisk
Beta measures volatility relative to the market. A beta of 1.5 means the stock moves 50% more than the S&P 500 in either direction.
Beta > 1 = more volatile than market
